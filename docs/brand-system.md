# docs/brand-system.md

Brand name: Northeast Technical Works (NTW)
Brand concept: Infrastructure in Motion
Positioning: The Northeast corridor’s most disciplined infrastructure deployment team.

Manifesto
NTW exists to deliver disciplined, inspection-ready field execution for security and network infrastructure across the toughest region in the U.S. Northeast.
We prioritize:
- Execution over promises
- Precision over shortcuts
- Documentation over guesswork
- Professionalism over chaos

Audience priorities
- Integrators: need reliable coverage, consistent workmanship, clean closeout packages.
- Enterprise IT: need uptime, standards, security, minimal disruption.
- Facilities/Property Ops: need scheduling discipline, cleanliness, tenant-safe work.
- Rollout managers: need repeatability, regional scale, and time-window execution.

Brand personality
- Luxury industrial: quiet confidence, not flashy.
- Technical elegance: systems-first, diagram-supported.
- Disciplined: calm urgency, operational clarity.

Voice and tone
- Confident and precise. Short sentences.
- Avoid buzzwords, hype, exclamation marks, and “revolutionary.”
- Use operational language: scope, schedule, deploy, verify, closeout.

Messaging pillars
1) Northeast Authority
   - NY/NJ/CT focused; understands dense infrastructure and tight schedules.
2) Inspection-ready Craft
   - clean installs, labeling, testing, documentation.
3) Repeatable Deployment Engine
   - rollout capability across multiple sites.
4) After-hours and cutover discipline
   - overnight/weekend windows where required.

Claims policy (proof-first)
Every major claim must be backed by at least one proof artifact module:
- photo examples (sanitized)
- sample closeout package pages (redacted)
- labeling standard snippet
- testing/certification summary
- process timeline
- coverage map

Brand tagline options
- “Infrastructure Deployment Done Right.”
- “Inspection-Ready Infrastructure.”
- “Northeast Field Execution, Disciplined.”

CTA language standards
Primary: Request Coverage
Secondary: Request Service
Tertiary: Download Capability Statement
Integrator-specific: Partner Intake

Customer promise (what we actually deliver)
- Clean installation and remediation
- Consistent documentation
- Scheduling reliability
- Professional onsite presence
- Clear closeout and handoff

What NOT to do
- Do not present as “handyman contractor.”
- Do not use playful icons or cartoon illustrations.
- Do not bury CTAs.
- Do not use generic stock “smiling electrician” photography.
